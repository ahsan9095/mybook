Common issues caused to boilers that require immediate attention

1. Dripping of Pipes and Leaking - These are identified by broken pipes or valves and could be detected and fixed only by professionals.

2. No Hot Water or Heat - You will easily know when this occurs, but the reasons could be due to low water levels or even a broken diaphragm. A qualified heating engineer should attend to it to identify and replace any faulty parts.

3. Unusual Noises from the Boiler  - You may hear gurgling or bubbling sounds from your boiler and that may not be a serious issue. However, getting them checked will help further damage to the internal parts of your boiler such as the heat exchanger.

4. The Pilot Light doesn't switch on - This may be due to minor causes such as clogged up residue at the pilot light preventing it from lighting. 5. Thermostat issues - An issue with the thermostat can be detected by it turning on or off on it own in the wrong times. A thermostat replacement can fix the problem.

6. Pressure loss - A faulty pressure release valve or a water leak could cause this issue.

7. Boiler turns itself off - This could be quite annoying because you may have to manually switch it on constantly and it would turn off on its own probably due to a number of reasons namely, pump issue, thermostat failure, poor water circulation and so on. Only an experienced engineer would be able to diagnose the issue and give you a solution.
